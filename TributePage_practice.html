Henry Ford

Timeline of Henry Ford's career:

  • 1863 - Ford was born in a farmhouse in Springwells Township, Michigan.
  • 1879 - Left home at the age of 16 to find work in Detroit.
  • 1890s - He began repairing and later constructing engines, and through the 1890s worked with a division of Edison Electric.
  • 1903 - He founded the Ford Motor Company after prior failures in business, but success in constructing automobiles.
  • 1908 - The introduction of the Ford Model T automobile in 1908 is credited with having revolutionized both transportation and American industry. As the sole owner of the Ford Motor Company, Ford became one of the wealthiest persons in the world.
  • 1912-1914 - Ford was known for his pacifism during the first years of World War I, although during the war his company became a major supplier of weapons.
  • 1945 - He turned over the company to his grandson Henry Ford II in 1945.
  • 1947 - Upon his death in 1947 he left most of his wealth to the Ford Foundation, and control of the company to his family.
